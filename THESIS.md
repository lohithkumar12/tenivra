30-second company explanation

“We are building a software platform for service businesses like clinics. Each business signs in, defines its services, rules, pricing, and availability, and our system turns that into customer-facing automation like booking, Q&A, reminders, and scheduling. Instead of building custom software for every clinic, we offer one product that each clinic configures for itself.”

0.1 One-page thesis
Working thesis

Problem
Small service businesses still run a lot of customer operations manually: answering repeated questions, taking bookings, handling reschedules, sending reminders, collecting basic intake info, and following business rules. Most existing tools solve only one part, like booking or chat, and custom software is too expensive for small businesses.

Who pays
The business pays, not the end customer. Specifically, clinic owners or operators pay a monthly subscription because your product saves staff time, reduces missed bookings, and improves customer response speed.

Why now
AI and workflow tools are now good enough to turn structured business information into useful automation without building custom software for every business. At the same time, service businesses are under pressure to improve responsiveness without hiring more staff. That makes “configure once, automate many workflows” much more practical now than a few years ago.

Why you
You already have the right technical shape for this: backend systems, FastAPI, AI/tool-calling, workflow thinking, and product-minded engineering. You are closer to building an automation-first SaaS than most developers.

Product
A multi-tenant SaaS where each business:

creates an account
defines services, rules, durations, pricing, FAQs, policies
connects basic channels or uses a hosted widget
gets customer-facing automation from that setup

Initial wedge
Start with one vertical only: clinics.

Monetization
Monthly subscription per business, with usage tiers. Setup fee can come later. The buyer is paying for saved admin work, not for “AI” itself.

Why clinics first
They have clear workflows, repeated customer questions, appointment logic, reminders, and intake rules. The pain is obvious and the ROI is easier to explain than in many other verticals.
